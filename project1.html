📊 Executive Summary

Jozi Pizza Co., a local pizza delivery service, was experiencing rising customer complaints and inconsistent delivery times that were hurting customer retention. With a 30-minute delivery target frequently missed and an 7.2% complaint rate (4× industry standard), the owner needed data-driven insights to identify root causes and implement cost-effective solutions.

I conducted a comprehensive operational analysis of 12,780 orders over 3 months, identifying specific bottlenecks in both geographic delivery and kitchen quality control. The analysis revealed actionable insights that could reduce delivery times by 32% and eliminate 85% of quality complaints through targeted staff training—requiring minimal capital investment.

🎯 The Business Problem

Challenges Faced:

  • Inconsistent Delivery Times: 30-minute target missed 39% of the time
  • Rising Complaints: 7.63% complaint rate (target: <2%)

  • Resource Constraints: Limited budget for solutions
💡 Client Quote:

"We sometimes get complaints even when delivery is fast. Our team is hardworking, but I don't know where the bottlenecks are. Would love ideas that don't cost a fortune to implement."

🔍 My Approach

1. Data Collection & Cleaning

Started with raw operational data containing 12,780 order records with 24+ variables including timestamps, staff assignments, delivery areas, and complaint details.

  • Cleaned inconsistent data entries (standardized text values)
  • Removed 7 incomplete records
  • Created calculated fields (total prep time, on-time flags, time period categories)
  • Built dimension tables for proper data modeling (Date, Area, Staff)

2. Exploratory Analysis

Investigated multiple dimensions to find patterns:

  • Geographic: Delivery time by area (5 zones: A, B, C, D, E)
  • Temporal: Peak hours, day-of-week trends, weekly patterns
  • Operational: Kitchen processes (prep, cooking, boxing times)
  • Quality: Complaint types correlated with staff performance
  • Staff Performance: Individual contribution to delays and errors

3. Dashboard Development

Created an interactive Power BI dashboard featuring:

  • Executive KPI summary with target comparisons
  • Geographic heatmap of delivery performance
  • Time-series analysis of order volume and complaints
  • Staff performance metrics with drill-down capability
  • Root cause analysis visuals

Technologies Used:

🔎 Key Insights Discovered

1. Geographic Concentration of Delays

  • Areas E and C: Averaged 48+ minutes (60% over 30-min target)
  • Areas A and B: Consistently met target at ~40 minutes
  • Impact: 47.3% of "late delivery" complaints from just 2 areas
  • Root Cause: Suboptimal delivery routes, not kitchen delays

2. Staff-Specific Quality Issues (Critical Finding)

  • 99.7% of "Too Doughy" complaints linked to one oven operator (Chilli)
  • 94.4% of "Wrong Size" errors linked to one order taker (Dineo)
  • Analysis revealed: Chilli's avg cooking time was 0.8 min shorter than peers
  • Implication: Training gap, not equipment failure

3. Peak Hour Capacity Constraints

  • Order volume: Tripled between 6-7 PM, especially Fridays
  • Performance impact: Delivery times and complaint rates both doubled during peak
  • Pattern: Kitchen could handle volume, but delivery capacity was insufficient
  • Insight: Need surge capacity, not permanent staffing increase

4. Process Efficiency Breakdown

  • Average total time: 44 minutes (47% over target)
  • Kitchen time: ~12 minutes (efficient)
  • Delivery leg: ~32 minutes (opportunity for optimization)
  • Bottleneck: Route efficiency, not kitchen speed

💡 Data-Driven Recommendations

Immediate Actions (Week 1-2):

1. Targeted Staff Retraining

Action: One-on-one coaching for Chilli (oven) and Dineo (order taker)

  • Implement daily complaint tracking for 14 days
  • Provide size-specific cooking time reference card
  • Shadow training with best-performing staff

Expected Impact: Near-elimination of two top complaint types (85% reduction)

Cost: Minimal - training materials only

2. Route Optimization

Action: Redesign delivery routes for Areas E and C

  • Assign experienced drivers to problem areas during peak
  • Test new routes during weekend peaks
  • Implement GPS tracking for route verification

Expected Impact: 20% reduction in delivery time for problem areas

Cost: Low - route planning time only

Short-Term Improvements (Week 3-4):

3. Peak Hour Staffing Adjustment

Action: Add temporary capacity 5:30-7:30 PM on Fridays & Saturdays

  • Shift prep work to slower hours (2-4 PM)
  • Add one part-time driver for evening peaks

Expected Impact: Eliminate order backlog during rush hours

Cost: Moderate - part-time labor

4. Performance Dashboard Implementation

Action: Weekly Power BI dashboard tracking key metrics

  • On-time delivery % by area and driver (target: >90%)
  • Complaint rate per staff role (target: <1%)
  • Average prep time during peak hours (target: <12 mins)

Expected Impact: Early issue detection and data-driven management

Cost: None - using existing Power BI license

📚 Key Takeaways

What I Learned:

  • Data quality matters: Spent 30% of project time on data cleaning— critical for accurate insights
  • Simple visuals win: Clear bar charts with reference lines often more impactful than complex visualizations
  • Context is key: Numbers mean nothing without business context and targets for comparison
  • Actionability over complexity: Stakeholders want clear next steps, not just impressive analytics
  • Tell the story: Data analysis is only valuable if you can communicate insights clearly to non-technical audiences
